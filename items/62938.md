## Summary

Keep open. Current `main` still resolves exec-approvals through legacy `~/.openclaw` paths instead of the shared `resolveStateDir()` path logic, and the broader hardcoded-path drift called out in review is still present.

## Evidence

- **Shared state-dir contract:** `resolveStateDir()` is the repo-wide state-path helper and explicitly prefers `OPENCLAW_STATE_DIR` when set, so exec-approvals should follow that contract to stay aligned with the rest of OpenClaw state.
  - file: [src/config/paths.ts:60](https://github.com/openclaw/openclaw/blob/245451b6a9c529464e177afa65013adca0d63eb5/src/config/paths.ts#L60)

  - sha: [245451b6a9c5](https://github.com/openclaw/openclaw/commit/245451b6a9c529464e177afa65013adca0d63eb5)
- **Main still uses legacy exec-approvals paths:** On current `main`, `resolveExecApprovalsPath()` and `resolveExecApprovalsSocketPath()` still expand hardcoded `~/.openclaw/exec-approvals.json` and `.sock` defaults instead of joining under `resolveStateDir()`. That means the PR's requested behavior is not implemented on main.
  - file: [src/infra/exec-approvals.ts:183](https://github.com/openclaw/openclaw/blob/245451b6a9c529464e177afa65013adca0d63eb5/src/infra/exec-approvals.ts#L183)
