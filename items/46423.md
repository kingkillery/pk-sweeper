## Summary

Keep open. Current `main` still does not implement the recurring transient-retry behavior this PR targets, so there is no strong basis to close it as already fixed or obsolete. The PR appears directionally useful, but part of its approach overlaps the currently documented one-shot-only `cron.retry` contract and should be reviewed on the merits rather than auto-closed.

## Evidence

- **Recurring jobs still wait for the natural schedule after errors on main:** In the recurring error branch, `main` always computes `backoffNext`, then schedules the later of `normalNext` and `backoffNext`. For long-interval `cron`/`every` jobs, that means the natural next slot still dominates, so transient failures do not get an early retry.
