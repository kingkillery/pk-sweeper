## Summary

Keep open. Current `main` at `7875092f4ddc6f2dbe218bea8506c9ac1da87c39` still lacks MiniMax `providerAuthAliases` metadata even though auth resolution and env-var reuse now depend on manifest alias mappings. MiniMax still exposes CN variant ids (`minimax-cn`, `minimax-portal-cn`) at runtime, and the PR's proposed manifest aliases plus regression test match documented and existing bundled-plugin patterns rather than duplicating already-shipped behavior.

## Evidence

- **MiniMax manifest on current main has no providerAuthAliases block:** `extensions/minimax/openclaw.plugin.json` declares `providerAuthEnvVars` and `providerAuthChoices`, but there is no `providerAuthAliases` entry for the CN variants anywhere in the manifest header.
  - file: [extensions/minimax/openclaw.plugin.json:8](https://github.com/openclaw/openclaw/blob/7875092f4ddc6f2dbe218bea8506c9ac1da87c39/extensions/minimax/openclaw.plugin.json#L8)

- **Auth alias resolution is manifest-driven:** `resolveProviderAuthAliasMap()` only learns provider auth aliases from `plugin.providerAuthAliases` and deprecated auth-choice ids, then `resolveProviderIdForAuth()` uses that map for auth/profile matching. Without explicit MiniMax CN aliases in the manifest, `minimax-cn` and `minimax-portal-cn` do not canonicalize here.
  - file: [src/agents/provider-auth-aliases.ts:83](https://github.com/openclaw/openclaw/blob/7875092f4ddc6f2dbe218bea8506c9ac1da87c39/src/agents/provider-auth-aliases.ts#L83)

- **Env-var reuse for aliased providers also depends on the alias map:** `resolveManifestProviderAuthEnvVarCandidates()` copies env-var candidates from canonical providers onto aliases only after calling `resolveProviderAuthAliasMap()`. If the MiniMax CN aliases are missing from the manifest, generic auth env lookup will not inherit the canonical MiniMax env vars through this path.
  - file: [src/secrets/provider-env-vars.ts:100](https://github.com/openclaw/openclaw/blob/7875092f4ddc6f2dbe218bea8506c9ac1da87c39/src/secrets/provider-env-vars.ts#L100)
